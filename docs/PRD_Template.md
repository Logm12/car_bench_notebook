# Product Requirements Document (PRD) Template

> **How to use this template**

This Product Requirements Document (PRD) template helps teams build **customer‑centric, outcome‑driven products** — and works equally well for **humans and AI prototyping tools**.

At first glance, it’s intentionally lightweight and easy to fill out. Each section includes a **toggle with guidance** explaining *why the section exists*, *what to include*, *what good looks like*, and *common pitfalls to avoid*.

👉 **New to PRDs?** Open the toggles as you write — they’re there to coach you.
👉 **Experienced PM?** Ignore the toggles and focus on the content.
👉 **Using AI tools?** Make sure **Problem Alignment, Target Users, Key Capabilities, UX Principles, Constraints, and Success Metrics** are filled in — these sections give AI the context and guardrails it needs to produce realistic outputs.

# Start by changing your PRD title

### Suggested Format: **[PRD] Description of Product Initiative**

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Ensure anyone scanning a list of documents immediately understands what this PRD is about.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - The fact that this is a PRD
    - A clear description of the product initiative
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - “[PRD] B2B Merchant Dashboard Navigation Redesign”
    - “[PRD] Improving Checkout Conversion for Ecommerce Merchants”
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Code names or internal acronyms
    - Vague titles like “Dashboard Improvements”

## Problem Alignment (or Opportunity)

[Clearly describe the core customer problem or opportunity this initiative addresses.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Explain what’s broken or missing today, why it matters to customers, and why it matters to the business.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - The customer problem in plain language
    - Evidence of the problem (customer feedback, behavioral data, support tickets)
    - Business impact if the problem is not addressed
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Clearly customer-centric
    - Grounded in real data and feedback
    - Easy to understand without design or technical jargon
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Describing symptoms without root causes
    - Jumping to solutions too early

### Why Now

[Explain why this problem should be solved now rather than later.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Justify timing and priority.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Upcoming launches, roadmap dependencies, or strategic priorities
    - Market or customer expectation shifts
    - Cost of delaying this work
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Time-bound and specific
    - Clear consequences of inaction
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Repeating the problem statement
    - Saying “this has always been an issue” without urgency

### Background & Evidence

[Summarize relevant research, data, and insights.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Provide credibility and context for the problem.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Customer interviews or survey insights
    - Quantitative data (usage, conversion, retention, revenue)
    - Market or competitive context
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Patterns, not anecdotes
    - Metrics that clearly support the problem
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Data dumps without interpretation
    - Including unrelated research

## Solution Summary

[High-level summary of the proposed solution and overall direction.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Describe the approach to solving the problem without diving into detailed requirements.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - The overall direction
    - Key principles or decisions
    - Assumptions the solution depends on
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Can be explained in under 60 seconds
    - Clearly maps back to the problem
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Turning this into a feature list
    - Over-specifying implementation

### Target Users

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Clarify who this solution is for — and who it is not.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - **Primary users, Secondary users,** and **Explicitly not for**
    - Real personas or roles
    - Clear exclusions to prevent scope creep
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Specific and opinionated
    - Prevents over-design
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Trying to serve everyone

### Definition of Success

[List 3–5 outcome-focused success metrics.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Define what success means in terms of outcomes, not outputs.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Customer outcome metrics (e.g., time saved, task success)
    - Business metrics (e.g., adoption, retention, revenue)
    - Baselines when available
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Metrics the team can influence
    - Clear connection to customer and business value
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Vanity metrics
    - Output-based goals (e.g., number of features shipped)

### UX / Design Principles

[List 3–5 principles guiding how the product should feel and behave.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Guide consistent design decisions for humans and AI tools.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Emotional tone
    - Interaction philosophy
    - Accessibility baseline
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Short, directive principles
    - Influences real tradeoffs
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Generic statements like “user-friendly”

## Scope & Capabilities

[One-paragraph TL;DR defining what is in scope and what is explicitly out.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Set clear boundaries and protect focus.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - What is included in this initiative
    - What is intentionally excluded
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Easy to reference during planning
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Repeating the solution summary

### Key Capabilities (AI + Human Friendly)

[List outcome-based capabilities the system must support.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Translate the solution into capabilities that AI tools can interpret without guessing.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Simple, testable capability statements
    - No UI or technical details
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Each capability maps to user value
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Writing technical requirements

### In-Scope: Detailed User Stories

[Prioritized user stories using real personas.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Align the team on user value and expected behavior.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Persona-based stories
    - Outcome-focused language
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Specific and testable
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Implementation details disguised as stories

### Out-of-Scope

[List features intentionally excluded or deferred.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Make tradeoffs explicit.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Known exclusions
    - Deferred ideas
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Clear reasoning for exclusions
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Leaving this empty

## Delivery, Risks & Open Questions

### Release Plan & Milestones

[When does this ship, how will it roll out, and how will outcomes be evaluated?]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Align on timing, phases, and how outcomes will be measured post-launch.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Key milestones
    - Phased rollout or experiment plan
    - How success metrics will be reviewed
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Outcome-driven milestones
    - Clear checkpoints
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Shipping dates without validation plans

### Constraints & Assumptions

[List known constraints and assumptions.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Define boundaries that shape the solution and AI outputs.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Technical, legal, or resource constraints
    - Key assumptions
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Explicit and realistic
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Leaving assumptions implicit

### Open Questions & Risks

[List unresolved questions, risks, and dependencies.]

- 🧭 *Instructional guidance*
    
    **Purpose**
    
    Surface uncertainty early and reduce false confidence.
    
    **What to include**
    
    - Open questions
    - Risks
    - Dependencies
    
    **What good looks like**
    
    - Honest and actively revisited
    
    **Common pitfalls**
    
    - Treating this as a parking lot
